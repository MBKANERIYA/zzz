Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between an ABN and a TFN?

A TFN (Tax File Number) is your personal tax identification number — you need it for employment, tax returns, and financial matters. An ABN (Australian Business Number) is for running a business — it lets you invoice clients, register for GST, and identify your business to the ATO. Most business owners need both.

Do I need an ABN if I'm a freelancer?

Yes. If you're operating as a freelancer or contractor in Australia, you need an ABN to invoice clients. Without one, businesses that pay you may withhold tax at the top marginal rate.

How long does it take to get an ABN?

In most cases, your ABN is issued immediately or within one business day after we submit the application. More complex applications (e.g., trusts or partnerships) may take up to two business days.

Can you help me choose the right business structure?

Absolutely. Before we register your ABN, we'll discuss your expected income, risk profile, and growth plans to recommend the most suitable structure — sole trader, partnership, company, or trust.

I'm new to Australia — can you help me get a TFN?

Yes. We regularly assist new migrants, international students, and working holiday visa holders with TFN applications. We'll walk you through the process and handle the submission for you.

Do I need to register for GST at the same time?

You must register for GST if your annual turnover is $75,000 or more. If you're below the threshold, registration is optional but can be beneficial — we'll advise whether it makes sense for your situation.
