Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a sole trader and a company?

A sole trader is the simplest structure — you and the business are the same legal entity. A company is a separate legal entity, which offers asset protection and potential tax advantages, but comes with more compliance obligations. We help you weigh the trade-offs based on your specific situation.

How long does it take to register a company?

Most registrations are completed within 1 to 3 business days. Once registered, you'll receive your ACN (Australian Company Number), and we'll apply for your ABN and TFN at the same time.

Do I need a Director ID?

Yes. All company directors in Australia are required to have a Director Identification Number. This is a one-time application, and we'll guide you through the process.

Can you help me restructure an existing business?

Yes. If you're currently operating as a sole trader and want to move to a company or trust structure, we can manage the transition — including any tax implications, ABN transfers, and new registrations.

How much does company setup cost?

Our fees depend on the complexity of the structure. A straightforward company registration is typically more affordable than setting up a trust or a multi-entity structure. Contact us for a quote based on your needs.

What ongoing obligations will I have after setup?

Companies must lodge annual tax returns, pay an annual ASIC review fee, maintain proper financial records, and meet BAS and PAYG obligations if registered for GST or employing staff. We can manage all of this for you on an ongoing basis.
