Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I get financial reports prepared?

It depends on your business size and complexity. Most growing businesses benefit from monthly or quarterly reports. At minimum, annual financial statements are required for tax lodgement.

What's the difference between bookkeeping and financial reporting?

Bookkeeping is the day-to-day recording of transactions. Financial reporting takes that data and turns it into structured statements — profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow — that tell you how your business is performing overall. We offer both services.

Can you prepare reports for my bank or investors?

Yes. We regularly prepare financial statements for loan applications, investor presentations, and business valuations. Our reports are prepared in accordance with Australian Accounting Standards.

Do I need financial reports if I'm a sole trader?

Even sole traders benefit from understanding their profitability, cash flow, and expense patterns. Regular reporting helps you make better decisions about pricing, spending, and growth — regardless of your business structure.

What accounting software do you work with?

We work with Xero, MYOB, and QuickBooks. If you're using another platform, contact us and we'll confirm compatibility.

Are you based in Sydney?

Yes. TaxFlow Accounting Services is based in Sydney's Harris Park area. We prepare financial reports for businesses across Sydney and also work with clients Australia-wide through our cloud-based platforms.
